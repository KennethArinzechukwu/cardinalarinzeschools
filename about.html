About Us

Br. Charles Okeke, Superior General 2009 – 2015, had intended to open a befitting Secondary School for the Congregation of the Brothers of St. Stephen but before he could do that he thought it was wise to open a pre-secondary first from which the graduates will be admitted to secondary, hence the founding of St. Stephen’s Nursery and Primary. In December 2013, at the general meeting of the Brothers at Nkwelle – Ezunaka, Br. Charles Okeke with the consent of his councilors constituted a vibrant committee with the mandate to fence the novitiate compound and later a mandate to open a Secondary school was included. Br. Patrick Okaka the then project manager was made the chairman of the committee. Other members included: Br. Samuel Obijuru, Br. Benedict Azebeokhai, Br. Joseph Onyekonwu, Br. Martin Ukanwa, and Br. Emeka Maduka. The committee later coopted magistrate Ngozi Okoye and Rev. Fr. Theophilus Odukwe as members. The committee worked assiduously to see that the objective was achieved, and it was achieved. In August 2014, the committee with the consent of the Superior General organized a luncheon for Francis Cardinal Arinze and his friends as well as friends of the Brothers at the residence of Cardinal Arinze, the founder of the Brothers of St. Stephen. Some invited guests turned up and donated generously after listening to the address read by the Superior General. Senator Victor Umeh made a donation of five Million Naira (#5m) plus pledges and cash donations by other philanthropists present. With the money realized, the novitiate compound was fenced, and then foundation was started for Secondary School. Later in the year, the then governor of Anambra State, Chief Willie Obiano, attended an event at the novitiate, same day with Francis Cardinal Arinze, a ground breaking ceremony for the secondary school was made. By July 2015, first floor of the building had been decked and work was going on progressively.
The school founded by Br. Charles Okeke in 2013/2014, took off during the administration of Br. Pantaleon Okafor on the 19th September 2016 with twelve (12) students and six (6) teachers with a Mass celebrated by His Eminence Francis Cardinal Arinze. The pioneer principal remains Mr. Charles Onwuagana who was also present at the opening Mass together with the brothers. At the Mass His Eminence Francis Cardinal Arinze commissioned both the staff and students to swing into action towards achieving excellence in all spheres of life: academically, socially, morally, mentally, and otherwise. Since then the school has not left any stone unturned to achieve the desired result. Thanks to God, the school which began with 12 students and six teachers in 2016, had risen to 439 students and 34 staff in 2022/2023 academic session. The principal remains Mr. Charles Onwuagana, who is being assisted by two vice principals: vice administration and vice academics, and other principal officers like the Dean of studies.

Aims and Objectives

Our vision is inherent in our motto- KNOWLEDGE AND DISCIPLINE.

We aim to achieve these by;

 

    • Instilling the highest level of discipline in all aspects of development of the students. Placing emphasis on individual student as we recognize that each child is unique being capable and able to excel diligently and with integrity.

 

    • Exposing them to modern global teaching and learning facilities in order to prepare them for the world at large without them losing their cultural heritage.

 

    • Enlightening them through seminars and workshops where renowned external role models in society would serve as guest speakers to counsel, educate and develop them morally, socially, mentally and physically.

 

  • Giving them the best pastoral care. Being a full boarding community, students are developed personally and guided by trained staff to be responsible young people.

Mission, Vision and Philosophy

Our Mission

    • To produce students who are capable of contributing meaningfully by their moral and disciplinary-life style to better the society.

 

Our Vision

    • To appreciate and demonstrate the moral values needed to live as a Christian at the various stages of growth and development.

 

Conclusin

  • The motto, mission Statement as well as the vision statement tell more about the school, the intention of the founding fathers and what the students are intended to be for their personal growth and the good of the society. To the Glory of God!

 

LIFE AFTER CDMIS

  • In getting approval for external exams, the school management underwent rigorous processes from the government. The school later got approval and exam number to write the Basic Examination Certificate (BECE) in the school in 2019. The first BECE was written in the school and the student’s scored 100% passes. The management later proceeded to getting approval for senior secondary school. It was a success made by the then chairman of Education /Mission who doubled as manager, Br. Samuel Obijuru. On 3rd September 2021, Br Charles Okeke came in as re – elected Superior General. He paved the way for a successful approval for West African Examination (that is, senior secondary certificate exam SSCE) and National Examination Certificate (NECO) and their examination numbers respectively from the Anambra State Ministry of Education. The approval for these external exams speak loud of the school as in the first SSCE and NECO exams the students recorded 100% passes just as in BECE, 2019.
